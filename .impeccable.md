## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters, engineering peers, and curious visitors. They arrive with little time and a skeptical eye — scanning for signal, not slogans. The site should prove depth quickly and leave a positive, personal reminder so they want to reach out.

### Brand Personality
**Playful · Transparent · Respectful.** Confident without peacocking; warm and human without being soft or vague. Emotionally: intrigue first, then trust — enough personality that they remember Marco, enough substance that they believe him.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Playful and bold**, never a generic CV that people bounce from. Dual theme (light + dark + system). Palette should be redefined around **blue and purple** as the starting point — current emerald/warm-neutral tokens are free to go. No fixed reference sites yet; anti-reference is the forgettable template portfolio.

Typography, motion, and layout should feel distinctive and owned — not “another dark developer site.” Keep accessibility as a baseline (keyboard, focus, contrast, reduced motion), not an afterthought.

### Design Principles
1. **Memorable over safe** — Prefer a bold, playful choice that feels like Marco over a polished but generic one.
2. **Depth on contact** — Surface personality immediately; reward a closer look with real craft and substance.
3. **Transparent & respectful** — Clear hierarchy, honest content, no gimmicks that obscure who he is or how to reach out.
4. **Blue–purple identity** — Build the system from blue/purple hues; tint neutrals toward that brand, keep accents rare and sharp.
5. **Accessible by default** — Meet common accessibility standards (contrast, focus, keyboard, reduced motion) without diluting the bold direction.

## Design Brief — Portfolio visual redesign

**Status:** Confirmed 2026-07-14

### Feature Summary
Full visual redesign of the single-page portfolio into one consistent product: playful polish on the current structure, blue–purple identity, coherent wording and spacing, fully responsive. Goal: leave people thinking *“I would like to work with that person.”*

### Primary User Action
Form a clear, positive impression of Marco and feel compelled to reach out.

### Design Direction
- **Approach:** Playful polish on the current SPA structure — not a hard layout teardown.
- **Palette:** Blue-tinted neutrals + rare sharp purple accent (OKLCH). Light + dark + system. No emerald; no purple→blue gradient text/fills.
- **Type:** Distinctive display + refined body (not Inter / Space Grotesk / JetBrains Mono).
- **Memorable hook:** Personality + craft that signals “great to work with.”

### Section Order
Hero → About → Experience → Projects → Skills → Contact

### Layout Strategy
Strong first viewport (name as brand, one sharp line, clear contact CTA) → human depth (About) → credibility (Experience) → craft proof (Projects) → supporting skills → easy Contact close. Varied spacing; cards only where interaction needs them. Command palette / theme / easter eggs kept in spirit, reshaped to the new system.

### Key States
Default light/dark, system theme, reduced motion, mobile/tablet/desktop, keyboard/focus — all intentional and accessible.

### Interaction Model
Scroll discovery with restrained motion; header anchors + command palette; theme toggle; primary path = reach out. Easter eggs optional, never blocking.

### Content
Unified playful / transparent / respectful voice across sections; tighten uneven wording.
